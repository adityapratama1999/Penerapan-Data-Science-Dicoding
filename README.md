# Final project Data Science: Solving the Problems of Jaya Jaya Maju Company.
# business Understanding
Jaya Jaya Maju is a multinational company operating in various sectors and was founded in 2000. With over 1,000 employees spread across the country, the company has grown rapidly over time.

However, along with this growth, Jaya Jaya Maju faces challenges in managing its human resources. One major challenge is the high attrition rate (the ratio of employee turnover to total employee turnover), which has reached over 10%. This is a serious concern for management, as high attrition can impact productivity, recruitment costs, and organizational stability.
# Business problems
The business problems this project aims to address include:
*   A high attrition rate (>10%), which can disrupt the company's operational stability.
*   A lack of understanding of the key factors leading to employee turnover.
*   The absence of a visual monitoring system that can help HR proactively monitor attrition trends and factors.
# Project Scope
*   Conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand attrition patterns.
*   Identify key features or factors contributing to employee turnover using statistical and machine learning methods.
*   Develop an interactive business dashboard that HR managers can use to regularly monitor and analyze these factors.

# ConcuConclusion
1. The attrition rate is quite high, at 17%.
2. Employees aged 31–40 years old dominate the attrition rate. This indicates that productive age   groups actually experience high turnover.
3. Employees with low incomes (especially those below 5,000) show a higher tendency to leave the company.
4. Certain roles, such as Sales Executive and Laboratory Technician, contribute to higher attrition rates than others.
5. Employees who work overtime (Overtime = Yes) have a higher attrition rate than those who don't, indicating potential burnout.
6. Males show higher attrition rates than females, although the difference is not significant.
# Recommended Action Items
* Reduce overtime by managing workloads more equitably or adding staff to busy divisions.
* Provide compensation increases or benefits, especially for lower-income employees, if they meet targets or improve their performance.
* Identify job roles such as Sales Executive and Lab Technician and determine whether workload, stress, or lack of career development are contributing factors.
* Establish a reward and recognition system to increase employee motivation and engagement, especially for long-term employees.
